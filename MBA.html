The MBA in Entrepreneurship & Innovation at Nolan University is designed for builders. 📚 Curriculum Structure

Our 12-month, 60-credit MBA combines intensive core modules with a hands-on venture practicum. You'll learn, build, and launch — all within one year.

Term 1 • Foundation

15 Credits
  • ENT601: Entrepreneurial Mindset & Opportunity Recognition
    Identifying and validating business opportunities
  • ENT610: Venture Finance for Founders
    Financial modeling, unit economics, and fundraising
  • MKT620: Customer Discovery & Lean Startup
    MVP development, customer interviews, and pivoting
  • ENT620: Design Thinking for Innovation
    Human-centered design and creative problem-solving

Term 2 • Building

15 Credits
  • ENT630: Building & Leading Teams
    Hiring, culture, and leading high-growth organizations
  • ENT640: Product-Market Fit & Growth
    Scaling strategies, metrics, and growth hacking
  • LAW650: Legal Foundations for Startups
    Entity formation, IP, contracts, and cap tables
  • ENT660: Pitching & Storytelling
    Crafting compelling narratives for investors

Term 3 • Scaling

15 Credits
  • ENT710: Venture Capital & Alternative Financing
    VC landscape, term sheets, and negotiation
  • ENT720: Scaling Operations
    Supply chain, logistics, and operational excellence
  • ENT730: Innovation in African Markets
    Navigating the unique challenges of African markets
  • ENT740: Corporate Innovation & Intrapreneurship
    Driving innovation within established organizations

Term 4 • Launch

15 Credits
  • ENT800: Venture Practicum (Capstone)
    Launch your venture with mentorship and funding
  • ENT810: Investor Pitch Night
    Present to leading VCs and angel investors
  • ENT820: Global Innovation Tour
    Immersion in leading startup ecosystems
  • ENT830: Founder's Toolkit
    Practical skills for post-MBA journey

🚀 Venture Practicum — Your Capstone Experience

📖 Complete Course Catalog

Explore our comprehensive curriculum, designed by founders for founders.

ENT601: Entrepreneurial Mindset & Opportunity Recognition • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: This course explores the entrepreneurial mindset and the process of identifying and evaluating business opportunities. Students learn to recognize patterns, assess market needs, and validate ideas through customer discovery.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Develop an entrepreneurial mindset and identify personal strengths
  • Apply frameworks for opportunity recognition and evaluation
  • Conduct customer interviews and synthesize findings
  • Articulate a clear value proposition

Assessment: Opportunity Journal (30%), Customer Interviews (30%), Final Opportunity Pitch (40%)

ENT610: Venture Finance for Founders • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Financial literacy for entrepreneurs. Covers unit economics, financial modeling, valuation, and fundraising strategies tailored for early-stage ventures.

MKT620: Customer Discovery & Lean Startup • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Hands-on application of Lean Startup methodology. Students build MVPs, run experiments, and iterate based on customer feedback.

ENT630: Building & Leading Teams • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: The art and science of building high-performing teams. Topics include hiring, culture, motivation, and leading through growth stages.

ENT640: Product-Market Fit & Growth • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: MKT620 ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Achieving product-market fit and designing growth strategies. Covers metrics, channels, and scaling tactics for startups.

ENT710: Venture Capital & Alternative Financing • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: ENT610 ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Deep dive into the venture capital landscape, term sheets, valuation, and negotiation. Includes simulations and guest lectures from VCs.

ENT730: Innovation in African Markets • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Understanding the unique opportunities and challenges of building ventures in African markets. Case studies of successful African startups.

ENT800: Venture Practicum (Capstone) • 6 Credits

▼
📚 Capstone 📊 Prerequisite: All core courses ⏱️ 90 Contact Hours

Course Description: The culminating experience where students launch their ventures. Includes mentorship, workspace, and access to the Nolan Venture Fund. Final presentations to a panel of investors.

ENT810: Investor Pitch Night • 2 Credits

▼
📚 Practicum 📊 Prerequisite: ENT710 ⏱️ 30 Contact Hours

Course Description: Preparation for and participation in a live pitch event with leading venture capitalists and angel investors from across Africa.
