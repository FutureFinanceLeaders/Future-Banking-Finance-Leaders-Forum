📅 TERM 1 · FINANCIAL TOOLKIT · April – July 2026

Entrepreneurial Finance & Fundraising
MBA 610 · 8 credits
12 weeks · 35 contact hours
  • Master cap tables, term sheets, valuation, convertible notes, and SAFEs
  • Build investor-ready financial models and pitch decks
  • Fundraising mechanics: angel, seed, Series A, venture debt
  • Negotiation simulations with real term sheets
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Corporate Finance (FIN 201) — Students apply corporate valuation frameworks directly to startup financing. BSc Module: Financial Modelling (FIN 210) — Excel modelling skills from BSc become the foundation for startup projections.
Assessment: Term Sheet Analysis (30%), Financial Model (40%), Pitch Presentation (30%)
Entrepreneurial Mindset & Opportunity
MBA 620 · 8 credits
12 weeks · 35 contact hours
  • Opportunity recognition and customer discovery (50+ interviews)
  • Effectuation theory and creative problem-solving
  • Business model canvas and lean startup methodology
  • Identifying unmet needs in African markets
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Behavioural Finance (FIN 410) — Understanding investor psychology complements founder psychology. BSc Module: Strategic Management (MGMT 301) — Strategic frameworks from BSc inform market opportunity analysis.
Assessment: Customer Discovery Report (40%), Opportunity Analysis (60%)
Financial Accounting for Entrepreneurs
MBA 630 · 7 credits
12 weeks · 30 contact hours
  • Understanding financial statements: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow
  • Accounting principles for early-stage ventures
  • Cash flow management and burn rate analysis
  • Financial controls and internal controls for startups
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Financial Accounting (ACC 101) — Students deepen their understanding of accounting by applying it to startup contexts. BSc Module: Managerial Accounting (ACC 102) — Cost analysis skills directly transfer to startup unit economics.
Assessment: Financial Statement Analysis (40%), Case Study (60%)
Corporate Finance Fundamentals
MBA 640 · 7 credits
12 weeks · 30 contact hours
  • Capital budgeting and cost of capital for high-growth firms
  • Valuation basics: DCF, comparable analysis, multiples
  • Financing decisions: debt vs. equity for growth
  • Capital structure theories applied to startups
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Corporate Finance (FIN 201) — This MBA module builds directly on BSc corporate finance theories, applying them to entrepreneurial contexts. BSc Module: Investment Analysis (FIN 202) — Valuation techniques are reinforced and expanded.
Assessment: Valuation Project (50%), Final Exam (50%)

📅 TERM 2 · THE INVESTOR'S DEEP DIVES · August – December 2026

Venture Capital & Private Equity
MBA 710 · 8 credits
12 weeks · 35 contact hours
  • LBO modelling and fund economics (waterfalls, carry, management fees)
  • Deal sourcing, due diligence, and investment committee memos
  • LP-GP dynamics and fund structuring
  • Build a full fund model from scratch
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Alternative Investments (FIN 420) — PE/VC theory from BSc is applied through practical modelling. BSc Module: Mergers & Acquisitions (FIN 430) — Deal structures and valuations are directly applicable to VC/PE transactions.
Assessment: Fund Model (40%), Deal Memo (30%), Final Exam (30%)
Growth Strategy & Scaling Ventures
MBA 720 · 8 credits
12 weeks · 35 contact hours
  • Product-market fit: metrics, pivot vs. persevere decisions
  • Scaling strategies: unit economics, LTV:CAC, cohorts
  • Go-to-market execution and channel strategies
  • Growth frameworks (RICE, ICE, A/B testing)
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Strategic Management (MGMT 301) — Strategic frameworks from BSc inform scaling decisions. BSc Module: Marketing Analytics (MKT 310) — Data-driven marketing skills enhance growth strategy execution.
Assessment: Growth Strategy Report (50%), Case Analysis (50%)
Financial Modeling for Startups
MBA 730 · 7 credits
12 weeks · 30 contact hours
  • Three-statement models for high-growth ventures
  • Scenario analysis, sensitivity tables, and Monte Carlo simulations
  • Investor-ready projections and data room preparation
  • Advanced Excel for venture finance (VBA, power query)
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Financial Modelling (FIN 210) — Excel modelling skills from BSc are elevated to advanced startup applications. BSc Module: Quantitative Methods (MTH 205) — Statistical foundations support scenario analysis.
Assessment: Model Project (60%), Model Documentation (40%)
Deal Structuring & Negotiation
MBA 740 · 7 credits
12 weeks · 30 contact hours
  • Term sheet anatomy: valuation, liquidation preferences, anti-dilution
  • Valuation methodologies for early-stage companies
  • Negotiation tactics for founders and investors
  • Simulated deal negotiations with live feedback
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Investment Banking (FIN 401) — Deal structuring principles from BSc are applied directly. BSc Module: Corporate Finance II (FIN 202) — Valuation methods inform term sheet negotiations.
Assessment: Negotiation Simulation (50%), Term Sheet Analysis (50%)

📅 TERM 3 · CAPSTONE & SCALE · January – April 2027

Venture Practicum (Capstone)
MBA 810 · 15 credits
12 weeks · 60 contact hours
  • Launch a real venture or complete a comprehensive investment thesis
  • Full financial projections, unit economics, and fundraising strategy
  • Pitch deck development and Demo Day presentation to investors
  • Mentorship from successful founders and VCs
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Applied Investment Project (FIN 490) — Students can integrate capstones: build the financial model in BSc, launch the venture in MBA. BSc Module: Portfolio Management (FIN 310) — Investment principles inform venture strategy.
Assessment: Final Venture (60%), Pitch Presentation (40%)
Advanced Valuation & Exit Strategies
MBA 820 · 10 credits
12 weeks · 45 contact hours
  • DCF, comparable analysis, and LBO models for exits
  • M&A strategies: acquirer identification, process management
  • IPOs, direct listings, and SPACs
  • Exit planning for founders and investors
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: Mergers & Acquisitions (FIN 430) — M&A frameworks from BSc are applied to exit scenarios. BSc Module: Advanced Corporate Finance (FIN 301) — Advanced valuation methods are reinforced.
Assessment: Exit Strategy Report (50%), Valuation Model (50%)
Global Immersion (Elective)
MBA 830 · 5 credits
1 week intensive · 30 contact hours
  • One-week immersion in Silicon Valley, London, or Nairobi
  • Company visits: startups, VC firms, innovation hubs
  • Networking with founders, investors, and ecosystem leaders
  • Ecosystem mapping and comparative analysis
🔗 SYNERGY WITH BSc FINANCE & INVESTMENT
BSc Module: International Finance (FIN 320) — Global market perspectives complement immersion experiences. BSc Module: Global Financial Markets (FIN 330) — Understanding international capital flows enhances ecosystem analysis.
Assessment: Immersion Report (100%)
