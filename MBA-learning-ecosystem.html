📘 New Venture Finance · Theory Notes
1. Valuation FundamentalsWeek 1

Key Concepts:

  • Pre-money vs Post-money valuation: Pre-money is value before investment. Post-money = Pre-money + Investment. Example: £5M pre + £2M investment = £7M post. Investor owns 2/7 = 28.6%.
  • Discounted Cash Flow (DCF): Project future cash flows, discount at appropriate rate. For startups, high discount rates (30-50%) reflect risk.
  • Venture Capital Method: Terminal value ÷ expected ROI. If exit at £50M, target 10x return, post-money = £5M.
2. Term Sheets: Economic TermsWeek 2
  • Liquidation Preference: 1x non-participating is standard.
  • Conversion Rights: Preferred converts to common at IPO.
  • Anti-dilution: Weighted average vs full ratchet.
📈 Marketing for Startups · Theory Notes
Growth Marketing Framework

The Bullseye Framework: Identify 3 channels, test, focus on one that works.

  • Acquisition channels: Viral, paid, SEO, content, sales, affiliate, etc.
  • Metrics: CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost), LTV (Lifetime Value), payback period.
  • LTV/CAC ratio: >3 is healthy, >5 is exceptional.
Cohort Analysis for Marketing

Track retention and revenue by acquisition cohort. Identify which channels bring highest-quality users.

💰 VC & Private Equity · Theory Notes
LBO Modelling Basics

LBO uses debt to acquire company. Returns driven by:

  • EBITDA growth
  • Multiple expansion
  • Debt paydown

Target IRR typically 20-30%.

Fund Economics
  • Management fee: 2% of committed capital
  • Carried interest: 20% of profits
  • Waterfall distribution: Deal-by-deal vs whole fund
🎨 Design Thinking · Theory Notes
5 Stages of Design Thinking
  1. Empathize: Understand users through observation and interviews
  2. Define: Articulate the core problem
  3. Ideate: Brainstorm multiple solutions
  4. Prototype: Build quick, low-fidelity models
  5. Test: Get feedback and iterate
MVP Definition

Minimum Viable Product: the smallest thing you can build to test your riskiest assumption.

🤖 Technology Strategy · Theory Notes
Disruptive Innovation (Christensen)

Disruptive technologies start in low-end or new markets, then move upmarket. Incumbents ignore them because they don't serve current customers.

Platform Ecosystems

Network effects: value increases with more users. Multi-sided platforms (e.g., Uber, Airbnb) connect different user groups.
