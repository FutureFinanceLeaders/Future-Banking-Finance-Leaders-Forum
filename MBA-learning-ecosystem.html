📘 New Venture Finance · Theory Notes
1. Valuation FundamentalsWeek 1

Key Concepts:

  • Pre-money vs Post-money valuation: Pre-money is value before investment. Post-money = Pre-money + Investment. Example: £5M pre + £2M investment = £7M post. Investor owns 2/7 = 28.6%.
  • Discounted Cash Flow (DCF): Project future cash flows, discount at appropriate rate. For startups, high discount rates (30-50%) reflect risk.
  • Venture Capital Method: Terminal value ÷ expected ROI. If exit at £50M, target 10x return, post-money = £5M.
  • Scorecard Method: Compare to similar startups, adjust for team, market, product.
  • Berkus Method: Assign value to key success factors (up to £2M).
2. Term Sheets: Economic TermsWeek 2

Key Clauses:

  • Liquidation Preference: 1x non-participating is standard. 1x participating is founder-unfriendly.
  • Conversion Rights: Preferred converts to common at IPO or acquisition.
  • Anti-dilution: Weighted average (broad/narrow) vs full ratchet.
  • Dividends: Usually cumulative, rarely paid.
3. Term Sheets: Control TermsWeek 3
  • Board Composition: Typical: 2 founders, 2 VCs, 1 independent.
  • Protective Provisions: VC veto rights on major decisions.
  • Drag-Along: Forces minority shareholders to join a sale.
  • Right of First Refusal (ROFR): Investors can match any share sale.
🧠 Entrepreneurial Mindset · Theory Notes
Effectuation Principles

Saras Sarasvathy's 5 principles [citation:2]:

  • Bird-in-hand: Start with who you are, what you know, whom you know.
  • Affordable loss: Invest what you can afford to lose, not expected return.
  • Lemonade principle: Leverage surprises and contingencies.
  • Crazy quilt: Build partnerships and pre-commitments.
  • Pilot-in-the-plane: Focus on activities you can control.
Opportunity Recognition Framework

Three essential components: Pain points (customer problems), market gaps (underserved needs), and trends (technological, social, regulatory shifts) [citation:5].

📊 Data Analytics · Theory Notes
Cohort Analysis

Cohorts group users by acquisition date. Track retention, revenue, engagement over time. Key metrics: retention curve, LTV, CAC, payback period.

LTV formula: (Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Gross Margin) / Churn Rate

A/B Testing Fundamentals
  • Statistical significance (p < 0.05)
  • Minimum sample size calculation
  • Avoiding common pitfalls (novelty effect, selection bias)

🚀 Venture Creation Lab · Capstone Project
Project Overview

Based on real MBA capstone experiences [citation:3][citation:9], you will work in teams to develop a comprehensive venture plan. This is the culmination of your MBA, integrating all prior learning.

Phase 1: Problem Validation (Weeks 1-2)
  • Conduct 20+ customer discovery interviews [citation:5]
  • Develop problem-solution canvas
  • Deliverable: Problem validation report (3 pages)
Phase 2: MVP Development (Weeks 3-4)
  • Build working prototype (Figma, no-code, or simple app)
  • Conduct user tests with 5-10 potential users
  • Deliverable: Prototype + user test results
Phase 3: Business Model & Financials (Weeks 5-6)
  • Business Model Canvas
  • Unit economics (LTV, CAC, payback period)
  • 3-year financial projections (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow)
  • Cap table and fundraising plan
Phase 4: Pitch Preparation (Weeks 7-8)
  • Investor pitch deck (10-12 slides)
  • Pitch rehearsal and feedback sessions
  • Deliverable: Pitch deck + 5-minute video pitch [citation:2]
Demo Day (Week 9)

Present to a panel of investors, entrepreneurs, and faculty. Following Imperial College's model, you'll receive feedback from an esteemed panel [citation:9].

Final deliverable: Full venture package including all above components.
