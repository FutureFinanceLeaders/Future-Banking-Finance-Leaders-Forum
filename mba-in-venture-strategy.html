TERM 1 15 CREDITS · 4 MODULES 🏦 FINANCE FOUNDATIONS (GSBF)

Build the investor's toolkit. Master valuation, deal structuring, and the mechanics of venture capital. These modules are led by the Graduate School of Banking & Finance.

FIN701: Venture Finance I: Capital Markets & Valuation 4 credits
Financial analysis, valuation methods (DCF, comps, LBO), capital structure decisions, and financial modeling for ventures.
GSBF INVESTOR
FIN702: Venture Finance II: Deal Structuring & Term Sheets 4 credits
Term sheet negotiation, cap tables, liquidation preferences, anti-dilution, and governance rights. Simulated deal negotiations.
GSBF INVESTOR BUILDER
FIN703: Venture Capital & Private Equity 4 credits
Fund structures, LP-GP dynamics, fundraising, due diligence, portfolio management, and exit strategies.
GSBF INVESTOR
LAW701: Legal & Regulatory Environment 3 credits
Entity formation, intellectual property, securities law, contracts, and exit strategies for ventures (taught jointly).
JOINT INVESTOR BUILDER
📊 Typical weekly workload: 10-12 hours per finance module + 8 hours for legal = ~40 hours total. Foundation term establishes rigorous analytical skills.
TERM 2 15 CREDITS · 4 MODULES 🚀 VENTURE CREATION (ENT)

Learn the builder's craft. From opportunity recognition to scaling, these modules are led by the School of Entrepreneurship & Venture Creation.

ENT701: Opportunity Recognition & Validation 4 credits
Entrepreneurial mindset, customer discovery, MVP development, and value proposition design. Conduct 20+ customer interviews.
ENT BUILDER INNOVATOR
ENT702: Product-Market Fit & Growth 4 credits
Growth metrics, acquisition channels, retention strategies, and scaling tactics for early-stage ventures.
ENT BUILDER
ENT703: Building & Leading Teams 4 credits
Hiring, culture, motivation, and leading high-growth organisations. Case studies of African startups.
ENT BUILDER
ENT704: Entrepreneurial Marketing 3 credits
Growth marketing, brand building, and customer acquisition on a bootstrap budget.
ENT BUILDER
📊 Typical weekly workload: 12-14 hours (Opportunity Recognition) + 8-10 hours each for other modules = ~40 hours. Intensive field work and customer discovery.
TERM 3 15 CREDITS · 4 MODULES ⚙️ INTEGRATION & DEEP DIVES (JOINT)

The heart of the joint programme. Finance and entrepreneurship students learn together, integrating both perspectives through applied seminars and cross-disciplinary modules.

VS701: Entrepreneurial Finance 4 credits
Financial decision-making for early-stage ventures from both founder and investor perspectives. Taught by faculty from both schools.
JOINT INVESTOR BUILDER
VS702: Impact & ESG Investing 4 credits
Impact measurement, ESG integration, blended finance structures, and development finance in African markets.
JOINT INVESTOR INNOVATOR
VS703: Technology & Innovation Strategy 4 credits
Disruptive technology, IP strategy, platform ecosystems, and tech scouting for investors and builders.
JOINT INNOVATOR INVESTOR
VS704: Deal Lab & Investment Committee 3 credits
Weekly deal lab analysing live transactions. Culminates in a full investment committee simulation with real VCs.
JOINT INVESTOR BUILDER
📊 Typical weekly workload: Deal Lab (6-8 hours prep) + 8-10 hours per module = ~40-45 hours. Integration term requires both analytical rigour and creative thinking.
TERM 4 15 CREDITS · 3 MODULES 🎯 LAUNCH & CAPSTONE

Apply everything you've learned. Launch a real venture, complete an investment thesis, or lead a corporate innovation project. The capstone integrates all three identities: builder, investor, innovator.

VS710: Venture Practicum (Capstone) 8 credits
Choose your track: Launch a venture, develop an investment thesis, or lead a corporate innovation project. Full business plan, financial model, and investor pitch.
JOINT BUILDER INVESTOR INNOVATOR
VS711: Investor Pitch Night & Demo Day 4 credits
Prepare and deliver your pitch to a panel of leading VCs, angel investors, and ecosystem leaders. Receive real funding offers.
JOINT INVESTOR BUILDER
VS712: Global Innovation Immersion 3 credits
One-week immersion in a leading innovation hub (Silicon Valley, Singapore, or Nairobi). Meet founders, VCs, and ecosystem leaders.
JOINT INNOVATOR INVESTOR
📊 Typical weekly workload: Venture Practicum dominates (15-20 hours), plus pitch preparation and immersion activities. Capstone intensity prepares you for real-world launch.

🔄 Integration Seminars (Across All Terms)

In addition to core modules, students participate in ongoing integration seminars that bring the cohort together for applied learning.

Founder-in-Residence

Monthly sessions with successful founders sharing journey and answering questions.

Investor Panel Series

VCs and angels discuss current trends, deal flow, and investment criteria.

Venture Studio Immersion

Week-long intensive where mixed teams build and pitch ventures.
