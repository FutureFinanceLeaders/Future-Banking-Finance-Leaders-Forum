📚 Curriculum Overview

A rigorous 12-month journey combining entrepreneurial strategy with financial mastery. Designed for builders who want to master capital.

Term 1 • Foundation

Building the Entrepreneurial Mindset & Financial Toolkit

Entrepreneurial Mindset & Opportunity Recognition6 credits
Identify, validate, and evaluate business opportunities. Customer discovery, lean startup methodology, and opportunity assessment frameworks.
Financial Accounting for Entrepreneurs6 credits
Understand financial statements, accounting principles, and how to make data-driven financial decisions for early-stage ventures.
Corporate Finance Fundamentals6 credits
Capital budgeting, cost of capital, valuation basics, and financing decisions tailored for high-growth companies.

Term 2 • Building & Funding

From Idea to Investable Venture

Venture Finance & Fundraising6 credits
Sources of capital, pitch decks, term sheets, valuation methods, and investor negotiations for early-stage ventures.
Product-Market Fit & Growth Strategy6 credits
Achieving product-market fit, growth metrics, scaling strategies, and go-to-market execution.
Financial Modeling for Startups6 credits
Build sophisticated financial models, unit economics, scenario analysis, and investor-ready projections.

Term 3 • Scaling & Strategy

Managing Growth and Capital Structure

Advanced Corporate Finance & Capital Structure6 credits
Optimal capital structure, debt vs. equity, growth equity, and financing strategies for scaling ventures.
Leading High-Growth Teams6 credits
Building organizational culture, hiring for scale, leadership in high-growth environments, and founder transitions.
Valuation & Deal Structuring6 credits
DCF, comparable analysis, LBO models, term sheet negotiation, and deal structuring for founders and investors.

Term 4 • Capstone & Launch

Real-World Application

Venture Practicum: Launch Your Venture12 credits
Launch a real venture, raise capital, or complete a deep-dive investment thesis. Mentored by successful founders and investors.
Investor Pitch Night2 credits
Present your venture to a panel of leading venture capitalists, angel investors, and industry experts.
Global Innovation Immersion2 credits
Study tour to leading startup ecosystems (Silicon Valley, London, or Nairobi) with company visits and networking.

📖 Detailed Course Descriptions

Explore each course's learning outcomes, assessment methods, and key topics.

Entrepreneurial Mindset & Opportunity Recognition ENT601

▼
📚 Core Course • 6 Credits ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours 🎯 Prerequisite: None

Course Description: This course explores the entrepreneurial mindset and the process of identifying, evaluating, and validating business opportunities. Students learn to recognize patterns, assess market needs, and develop solutions that create value.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Develop an entrepreneurial mindset and identify personal strengths
  • Apply frameworks for opportunity recognition and evaluation
  • Conduct customer interviews and synthesize findings
  • Articulate a clear value proposition and business model

Assessment: Opportunity Journal (25%), Customer Discovery Report (35%), Final Opportunity Pitch (40%)

Venture Finance & Fundraising FIN610

▼
📚 Core Course • 6 Credits ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours 🎯 Prerequisite: Financial Accounting

Course Description: Comprehensive exploration of fundraising strategies for early-stage ventures. Topics include angel investing, venture capital, crowdfunding, revenue-based financing, and alternative funding sources. Students learn to build pitch decks, negotiate term sheets, and manage investor relationships.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Understand the landscape of startup financing
  • Build a compelling pitch deck and financial projections
  • Analyze and negotiate term sheets
  • Develop a fundraising strategy for a real venture

Assessment: Pitch Deck (30%), Term Sheet Analysis (30%), Fundraising Strategy (40%)

Financial Modeling for Startups FIN620

▼
📚 Core Course • 6 Credits ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours 🎯 Prerequisite: Corporate Finance Fundamentals

Course Description: Hands-on course in building financial models for startups and high-growth companies. Students learn to build three-statement models, unit economics, scenario analysis, and investor-ready projections using advanced Excel and financial modeling software.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Build dynamic financial models from scratch
  • Develop unit economics and sensitivity analyses
  • Create investor-ready financial projections
  • Use financial modeling to inform strategic decisions

Assessment: Modeling Assignments (40%), Final Model Project (60%)

Venture Practicum: Launch Your Venture CAP800

▼
📚 Capstone • 12 Credits ⏱️ 90 Contact Hours 🎯 Prerequisite: All Core Courses

Course Description: The culminating experience of the MBA program. Students launch a real venture, raise capital, or complete a comprehensive investment thesis. Working closely with mentors, students apply all learning from the program to a real-world project. Final presentations to a panel of investors.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Apply entrepreneurial and financial frameworks to a real venture
  • Navigate the challenges of launching and scaling a business
  • Present effectively to investors and stakeholders
  • Build a professional network in the entrepreneurial ecosystem

Assessment: Milestone Deliverables (40%), Final Presentation (60%)
