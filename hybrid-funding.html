Welcome to the Hybrid Funding System

Nolan University, in partnership with M. Lewis Capital Partners and AfriLeap Capital, offers a term-based hybrid funding system for undergraduate and postgraduate programs. This system allows students to:

  • Receive scholarships for tuition and academic excellence
  • Access grants for innovation, entrepreneurship, and project work
  • Obtain flexible, founder-style loans with deferred or income-linked repayment

The program is optional and designed to support students who want to combine academic achievement with entrepreneurial and impact-driven projects.

Funding Options

M. Lewis Capital Partners

Scholarships: Legacy-impact tuition scholarships for MBA and Bachelor’s students. Conditional on GPA and leadership or impact projects.

Loans: Flexible founder-style loans to cover tuition or living costs. Repayment can be deferred or income-linked.

AfriLeap Capital

Grants: Non-repayable grants for student innovation, prototypes, research, or entrepreneurial projects.

Loans: Flexible loans for tuition or living costs, tied to project milestones or revenue from ventures.

Nolan University Merit Scholarships

Term-based academic merit scholarships automatically applied for eligible students, reducing overall tuition and loan dependency.

How to Apply

Students interested in hybrid funding should follow these steps:

  1. Review eligibility criteria for M. Lewis, AfriLeap, and University Merit Scholarships.
  2. Prepare necessary documents: academic records, project proposals, and financial information.
  3. Submit applications through the Nolan University Student Portal.
  4. Await term-based approval for scholarships, grants, and loans.
  5. Comply with GPA and project milestone reporting for disbursement each term.

FAQs:

  • Q: Is hybrid funding mandatory?
    A: No, students can choose scholarships, grants, loans, or any combination.
  • Q: Can I apply for both M. Lewis and AfriLeap funding?
    A: Yes, the system allows layering multiple funding sources per term.
  • Q: Are loans interest-free?
    A: Founder-style loans are typically 0–3% interest with flexible repayment options.
